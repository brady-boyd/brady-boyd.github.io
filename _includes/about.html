About Brady

Most of my adult life, I have been an automotive sales professional. I dropped out of college at 20 to work for my dad, a general manager at a new Ford dealership at the time. There are few professions that allow someone that young to make a decent living, and for a long time, selling cars is what I thought I wanted to do.

Years later, my dad and I opened a used car lot together for which I ran the day-to-day operations. I foolishly assumed that being my own boss would make up for some of the burnout I was experiencing only to discover that it only exasperated the problems.

After taking some time off to take care of family member in the late stages of Parkinson's, I decided that my energy would be best spent going back to school in order to be able to go to work and do something I enjoy.
